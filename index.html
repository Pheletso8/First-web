Metal-tetraporphyrazine intercalated Mxene as electrocatalyst for oxygen reduction and evolution

Fossil fuels, the primary energy source for decades, contribute significantly to environmental pollution and climate change, driving the need for sustainable alternatives and efficient energy storage solutions. Renewable sources like solar and wind face intermittency challenges, exacerbating grid instability and highlighting the importance of technologies such as rechargeable zinc-air batteries (RZABs). These batteries rely on oxygen electrocatalysis—oxygen reduction (ORR) during discharge and oxygen evolution (OER) during charge—but suffer from sluggish kinetics and high overpotentials. Current platinum-based catalysts are costly and unstable, prompting exploration of affordable alternatives. This study investigates Fe- and Co-metallotetraporphyrazines intercalated into MXenes (Ti2CTx), leveraging their stable MN4 active sites, conductivity, and corrosion resistance to enhance ORR/OER efficiency. The composite's performance was evaluated via physicochemical characterization (UV-Vis, XRD, SEM) and electrochemical tests (CV, LSV, EIS, RDE), aiming to advance cost-effective, durable catalysts for next-generation energy storage systems.
